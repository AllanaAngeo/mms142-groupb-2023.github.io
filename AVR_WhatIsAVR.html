What is AVR?

Published by: Sophia Baltao | Date: December 15, 2023



Virtual Reality (VR) is a digitally produced, three-dimensional virtual space where users can engage, typically accessed through a computer that can project 3D information on a display. This display may consist of separate screens or a wearable device like a head-mounted display (HMD), complemented by user identification sensors (Jia & Hamad, 2022).


TWO TYPES OF VR


Non-immersive VR employs multiple screens positioned around the user to display virtual information. An illustrative instance of this is observed in driving or flight simulations, where the user, seated in a chair, is surrounded by screens, creating the sensation of being in the cockpit or driverâ€™s seat without complete immersion (Jia & Hamad, 2022).
Immersive VR involves utilizing a wearable display, such as an HMD, to monitor user movements and deliver VR information based on their position. This enables users to encounter the virtual environment from all angles, providing a 360-degree experience (Jia & Hamad, 2022).

AVR vs. AR

Virtual Reality employs a headset to immerse you in a computer-generated environment for exploration. In contrast, augmented reality differs by overlaying digital images onto the real world using a clear visor or smartphone, rather than transporting you to a virtual realm (GCF Global, n.d.).
Augmented Reality (AR) is a technology designed to enhance the user's perception by overlaying digital information onto a live view of the real world. Its objective is to broaden the real-world experience by incorporating images, sound, video, and other virtual elements (Cointelegraph, n.d.).
